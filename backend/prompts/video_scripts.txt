SCRIPT 1 — INSTAGRAM REEL (reel)
DURATION: 15-30 seconds
TONE: High energy, scroll-stopping, visually driven
STRATEGY:
- HOOK: The first 2-3 seconds must stop the scroll. Use a bold statement, a surprising fact, or a direct address. Examples: "This kitchen will make you never want to eat out again." / "4 beds, 3 baths, and this backyard — under $650K." / "Come inside."
- SHOTS: 4-6 shots maximum. Fast cuts. Each shot should be 2-4 seconds.
- VOICEOVER: Short punchy sentences. Match the energy of the visuals. No full paragraphs.
- CTA: One line. Direct. "Link in bio." / "DM me to tour." / "Comment your favorite room."
- platform: "Instagram Reel"
- duration_seconds: 25

---

SCRIPT 2 — TIKTOK (tiktok)
DURATION: 30-60 seconds
TONE: Conversational, story-driven, authentic — like the agent is talking to a friend
STRATEGY:
- HOOK: Open with a relatable moment or a curiosity gap. Examples: "I walked into this house and immediately thought of you." / "This is the one everyone's been waiting for in [city]." / "Let me show you something most people miss about this listing."
- SHOTS: 6-8 shots. Slightly slower cuts than Reel. Room to breathe.
- VOICEOVER: Conversational and specific. Reference actual features by name. Sound like a knowledgeable friend, not a salesperson.
- CTA: Warm and direct. "Follow for more listings like this." / "Drop a question below and I'll answer it." / "DM me — I'll get you in for a private tour."
- platform: "TikTok"
- duration_seconds: 45

---

SCRIPT 3 — YOUTUBE SHORT (youtube_short)
DURATION: 45-60 seconds
TONE: Informative, lifestyle-forward, slightly more polished than TikTok
STRATEGY:
- HOOK: Open with the strongest lifestyle statement about this property or neighborhood. Examples: "This is what $649,000 looks like in [city] right now." / "Solar panels, resort amenities, A-rated schools — let me walk you through it."
- SHOTS: 8-10 shots. Deliberate pacing. Each shot has time to land.
- VOICEOVER: Most detailed of the three. Include 2-3 specific property highlights, a neighborhood angle, and a lifestyle moment. Still conversational — not a spec sheet.
- CTA: Authoritative and clear. "Subscribe for weekly listings." / "Watch until the end and then call me." / "I'll leave the address in the description."
- platform: "YouTube Short"
- duration_seconds: 55

UNIVERSAL RULES FOR ALL 3 SCRIPTS:
- Never use fair housing protected class language (race, religion, national origin, familial status, disability, sex).
- Do not describe the neighborhood in terms of who lives there or who it is "perfect for" in demographic terms.
- Every hook must be platform-native — do not write the same hook for all three.
- Every voiceover must match the shot sequence provided. Reference specific rooms and features visible in the shots.
- Write shot directions as direct instructions to the agent. Use second person: "Walk toward...", "Pan across...", "Hold on..."
- Keep voiceover lines short enough to be spoken naturally while moving through a space.
- The just_sold placeholder convention does not apply here — video scripts are filmed at listing time only.
- Each script must feel like it was written specifically for this property, not a generic real estate template.
- NEVER lead with or emphasize utility and support features: garage, driveway, concrete surfaces, parking, laundry room, or storage. These do not stop the scroll or sell the lifestyle. Never open a hook or voiceover with these features.
- ALWAYS structure the video narrative around lifestyle-forward features in this priority order:
    1. Outdoor living — pool, spa, screened enclosure, lanai
    2. Primary suite — location, pool access, barn door, walk-in closet
    3. Kitchen — countertops, cabinetry, layout, flow
    4. Living spaces — fireplace, open floor plan, natural light, staircase
    5. Recent major upgrades — roof, A/C, flooring
    6. Exterior curb appeal — facade, landscaping, arrival experience
    7. Garage and parking only as a closing supporting detail if space permits
